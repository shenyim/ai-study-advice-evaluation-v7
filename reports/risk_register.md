# Risk Register

| Risk | Severity | Why it matters | Mitigation |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Simulated model outputs are mistaken for real benchmark results | High | It can make the project look dishonest or overclaimed | Label simulated results clearly and separate them from real model outputs |
| High-risk academic advice sounds confident but lacks verification | High | Students may make bad application, course, or career decisions | Add risk-control gates, review queues, and official-source verification language |
| Rubric scores are subjective | Medium | Single-rater scoring may reflect personal bias | Add annotation guide, multi-rater workflow, and disagreement analysis |
| Dataset is too small or too clean | Medium | It may not represent messy real student questions | Expand questions by category, background, ambiguity, and risk level |
| Dashboard over-focuses on average score | Medium | Average quality can hide dangerous failures | Use risk-weighted score, P0 rate, high-risk review rate, and case review |
| Privacy issue if real student questions are collected | High | Student data may contain personal information | Use consent, remove identifiers, and store only minimal necessary information |
| Product becomes just a chatbot | Medium | The HCAI-DS value is evaluation and governance, not only answer generation | Keep the core product as an evaluation layer with guardrails and review workflows |
