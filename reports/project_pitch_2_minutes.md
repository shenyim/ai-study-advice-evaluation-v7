# 2-Minute Pitch

This project asks a practical question: if students use AI for academic, research, or career advice, how do we know whether the answers are actually useful and safe enough?

I built a human-centered AI evaluation platform that supports simulated model answers, real model imports, and human-scored annotations. The platform scores answers across quality and risk-control dimensions, applies risk-aware weighting, compares model candidates, and turns failures into a review queue with priorities, owners, root-cause hypotheses, and recommended fixes.

The research side includes multi-rater annotation templates, completeness checks, inter-rater reliability metrics, rater bias analysis, bootstrap confidence intervals for model differences, and an honest final research report. The product side includes a Streamlit dashboard with an overview, leaderboard, real model evaluation, human annotation, statistical evidence, review queue, case review, launch readiness, and portfolio summary.

The project is intentionally careful about limitations. It is a portfolio and research demo, not a deployed student-advice product and not a broad benchmark of commercial LLMs. Its value is showing how I would design the evaluation system around real human risk, evidence quality, and product decision-making.
